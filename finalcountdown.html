Amendments

These are the four missing pieces needed to deliver Net Neutrality in the EU

1. Prevent network discrimination

Large deep-pocketed companies should not be allowed to buy vip-tickets to prioritise their content over traffic from the rest of the internet.

What EU Parliamentarians need to do: The criteria defining specialised services need to be clarified to avoid discrimination on the network.

2. Treat all traffic equally

All traffic should be treated equally over the internet so that both unencrypted and encrypted content are delivered as fast as possible.

What EU Parliamentarians need to do: The provisions on traffic management must be modified to ensure equal treatment of traffic. Traffic management needs to be transparent, targeted and in accordance with the law.

3. Freedom to ban zero-rating

ISPs should not be allowed to become gatekeepers and charge companies for the delivery their services. Anyone with a great idea should be able to reach internet users without having to pay a toll.

What EU Parliamentarians need to do: A provision needs to be added to enable Members states to ban those discriminatory practices.

4. Managing network congestion

ISPs should be allowed to manage traffic in case of congestion, only if it is taking place.

What EU Parliamentarians need to do: Rules on managing congestion needs to be clarified to avoid a situation where ISPs will be interfering with the network for discrimatory reason.
